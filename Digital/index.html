Bad Bunny's Grammy win is a win for Latinos Everywhere

By Anaya Roman

The 65th Annual Grammy Awards took place on February 5, 2023, in Los Angeles California. The night was filled with star studded performances and memorable moments. Puerto Rican artist Bad Bunny took home the Grammy for Album of the year for his album "Debi Tirar Más Fotos." Upon announcing his name Bunny was brought to tears in his seat.

    "Ice out,"said the Puerto Rican artist, also known as Benito Antonio Martinez as he opened up his speach followed by a unanimous crowd applause. The grammy win comes at pivotful moment where many Latino lives have been affected by the recent ICE raids. Mass protests continue in Minneapolis since 12 arrests have been made with the death of the Minneapolis citizen Renee Good and Alex Pretti.

      Bad Bunny's grammy win marks the first time a spanish language album has won Album of the year. This recognition of a spanish language album is a huge step on a global scale reminding us even in the dark of times there is a light.

      Bad bunny recieved about 19.8 billion streams on Spotify in 2025. Coming from the high of a grammy win Bad Bunny is set to take the stage at the Super Bowl Halftime show this Sunday.
